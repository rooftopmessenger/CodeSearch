## APPENDIX: METHODOLOGY

### What ELS Is

An Equidistant Letter Sequence (ELS) selects every N-th letter from a corpus of length L. The expected frequency of a W-letter word at any starting position and skip is approximately (1/A)^W where A = 22 (Hebrew consonants). Statistical significance is established when the Z-score — (observed hits − baseline mean) / baseline standard deviation — exceeds 3.0, corresponding to p < 0.0013 under the Monte Carlo null hypothesis.

### What HeBERT Scores Mean

HeBERT (`avichr/heBERT`, 768-dim) produces cosine similarity scores (0→1) between the ELS token's embedding and the surrounding verse context embedding. Scores near 0.5 indicate high contextual coherence. **All project scores are below 0.52**, consistent with the ELS hypothesis: spaced letters are by construction less semantically integrated than plaintext words. The score is a measurement of coherence, not of truth-value.

### What SemZ Means

SemZ = (match HeBERT score − μ_random) / σ_random, where the random distribution is generated by Monte Carlo corpus shuffling. Negative SemZ (the project norm) means the ELS context is less semantically coherent than random. **Positive SemZ is extremely rare** — two confirmed instances in 37,527+ entries: Proverbs 29:7 (SemZ=+3.67, token **תשצ**/2030) and Zechariah 2:4 (SemZ=+0.206, token **איראנ**/Iran). Positive SemZ is the archive's strongest confirmation signal.

### What the Duality Gap Means

The Duality Gap (0.3746) is the cosine distance between the Peace centroid and the Dark/Adversarial centroid in HeBERT 768-dim space. A gap below 0.4 indicates semantic overlap — the prophetic corpus does not encode peace and deception as distinguishable by surface semantics. This is not a model failure; it is a documented property of the Biblical Hebrew prophetic genre. The Islamabad Veil exploits this gap structurally.
